  About

Hi! My name's Nate, and I'm an internet addict. They say acceptance is the first step on the road to recovery. But seriously -- I love creating new ways of communicating and understanding information. I've been learning and building on the web for about as long as it's existed as a publicly accessible resource. I first heard the sound of a modem connecting to the internet back in 1989 when I was about six years old, and I've been hooked ever since.

After five years of honing my skills as a professional developer, I'm currently focusing on deepening my knowledge of geographical information systems, bidirectional real time communication channels (primarily WebSocket), and new kinds of web server software like Node.js to see if I can play a part in shaping the next generation of web applications.

I grew up in Los Angeles, California and Paris, France and had the good fortune of growing up trilingual, learning English, French and Spanish as a young boy. My Spanish isn't as good as it used to be but I'm still fully fluent in French, and I'm always up for practicing or learning a new language. I studied Philosophy and Linguistics at UC Santa Cruz and Web Design and Interactive Media at the Art Institute of Portland, earning a Bachelor of Arts from the former and a Bachelor of Science from the latter.

I've been living in Portland, Oregon for close to three years and I'm thoroughly enjoying the good coffee, beer and books and the blossoming technology industry that can be found here.

If you need to get in touch with me I'm active on twitter and IRC (you can sometimes find me at #pdxtech on irc.freenode.net as @ngoldman) and I also check my email regularly. If I don't respond right away, I'm probably buried under a pile of work, but I'll get back to you as soon as I can. Thanks for visiting!
